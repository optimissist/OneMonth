You may be asking yourself, Self, what did she learn? Well, let me tell you a bit about it...

Responsive web design is an easy yet inclusive approach to web design to ensure your site looks good on a variety of screen sizes. This means that with one HTML document and some special css, you can design a website that can look good at 1020px and 680px.

You may call me crazy. You might call this witchcraft. But it's really just responsive design. There are three key ingredients. First you need a flexible grid. This means the columns can expand or contract depending on the size of the screen. Second you need flexible media. This will adjust images so they look right on the page, no matter the size. Last but certainly not least, you need media queries. This is a way to tailor css to specific screens and sizes using the same html base.

By using media queries, a flexible grid and flexible images, you can develop a site that can look good on a Macbook and a Pixel.
